# Energy Consumption Analysis Using SQL

## Project Overview

This project analyzes global energy production, energy consumption, carbon emissions, GDP, and population data using SQL. The objective is to identify trends, compare countries, and understand the relationship between economic growth, energy usage, and environmental impact through analytical SQL queries.

## Objectives

* Analyze energy production, consumption, and emission trends across countries.
* Study the relationship between GDP, population, and energy usage.
* Generate meaningful insights using SQL-based data analysis.

## Technologies Used

* MySQL
* SQL (Joins, Aggregations, Subqueries, Window Functions)
* MySQL Workbench

## Database Schema

The project consists of the following tables:

* **Country** – Stores country information.
* **Emission_3** – Carbon emissions by country, year, and energy type.
* **Production** – Energy production data.
* **Consumption** – Energy consumption data.
* **GDP_3** – GDP data by country and year.
* **Population** – Population data by country and year.

## Analysis Performed

* Top countries by GDP
* Energy production vs. energy consumption
* Global emission trends
* Emission-to-GDP ratio
* Energy consumption per capita
* Energy production per capita
* Population and emissions analysis
* Global emission share by country
* GDP and energy production comparison
* Top emitting countries
* Energy consumption relative to GDP
* Global averages of GDP, emissions, and population

## Key Insights

* Global GDP, population, and carbon emissions showed an increasing trend from 2020 to 2023.
* China, the United States, and India were among the leading contributors to GDP and carbon emissions.
* Energy production and consumption generally increased together across countries.
* Fossil fuels remained the major source of global carbon emissions.
* Countries with larger economies and populations generally recorded higher energy consumption and emissions.

## SQL Concepts Used

* SELECT Statements
* INNER JOIN
* GROUP BY
* ORDER BY
* Aggregate Functions (SUM, AVG, MAX)
* Subqueries
* Window Functions (LAG)
* Mathematical Functions (ROUND)

## Challenges Faced

* Integrating data from multiple tables using country and year.
* Handling missing or inconsistent data.
* Writing optimized SQL queries for analytical reporting.
* Calculating accurate per-capita and ratio-based metrics.

## Conclusion

* Energy production, consumption, GDP, and emissions showed an overall increasing trend.
* Economic growth and population growth were associated with higher energy demand and emissions.
* SQL proved to be an effective tool for analyzing large datasets and generating meaningful insights.
